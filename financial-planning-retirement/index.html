5 Financial Moves Everyone Should Make Before Retirement

Michael Carrington — Northbound Report |March 25, 2026 |5 min read

Preparing for a Secure Retirement: Key Financial Planning Steps

Timing is Everything: When to Claim Social Security

When it comes to planning for retirement, timing is crucial, especially when it comes to claiming Social Security benefits. The age at which you claim your benefits can significantly impact your monthly payout and overall lifetime benefits. Generally, the earlier you claim your benefits, the lower your monthly payout will be. On the other hand, waiting until full retirement age can result in a larger monthly payout, but you'll have to wait longer to receive it. It's essential to consider your individual circumstances, life expectancy, and financial goals before making a decision.

Healthcare Costs: A Major Expense in Retirement

Healthcare costs are a significant concern for many retirees. Medical expenses can quickly add up, and without proper planning, they can deplete your retirement savings. It's crucial to factor in estimated healthcare costs when creating your retirement budget. Consider investing in a health savings account (HSA) or a Medicare supplement policy to help cover unexpected medical expenses.

Downsizing: A Practical Consideration for Retirement

Downsizing your living situation can be a practical consideration for retirement. As you age, you may need to adapt to a more modest lifestyle or relocate to a more affordable area. Consider the costs associated with maintaining your current home, such as property taxes, maintenance, and insurance. Downsizing can help you free up resources for more important expenses, like healthcare and travel.

Building an Emergency Fund: A Safety Net for Retirement

A well-stocked emergency fund is essential for any retiree. Unexpected expenses, such as car repairs or medical bills, can arise at any time, and without a cushion of savings, you may be forced to dip into your retirement accounts. Aim to save 3-6 months' worth of living expenses in an easily accessible savings account.

Diversifying Your Income: A Smart Retirement Strategy

Income Diversification: A Key to a Secure Retirement

In today's uncertain economic climate, it's more important than ever to diversify your retirement income streams. Relying on a single source of income, such as a pension or Social Security, can leave you vulnerable to market fluctuations or benefit cuts. Consider investing in a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, and other asset classes to generate passive income in retirement.

Here are some practical steps you can take to diversify your retirement income:

  • Invest in a tax-efficient brokerage account to generate long-term growth.
  • Consider renting out a spare room or property on Airbnb to supplement your income.
  • Explore alternative sources of income, such as a part-time job or freelance work.
  • Invest in a variable annuity or other guaranteed income product to provide a predictable source of income.

By following these essential financial planning steps, you can create a secure and sustainable retirement income strategy. Don't wait until it's too late to start planning – take control of your financial future today.

Conclusion: A Secure Retirement is Within Reach

Planning for retirement can seem overwhelming, but by focusing on key financial planning steps, such as timing Social Security claims, managing healthcare costs, downsizing your living situation, building an emergency fund, and diversifying your income, you can create a secure and sustainable retirement. Remember, every small step counts, and with the right guidance and resources, you can achieve your retirement goals.
