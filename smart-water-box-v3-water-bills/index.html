Rising Water Bills: The Hidden Cost of Inflation

As Americans grapple with the ongoing effects of inflation, many households are facing a surprising new expense: skyrocketing water bills. In cities across the country, water rates are on the rise, leaving families scrambling to make ends meet and find ways to reduce their monthly expenses.

The Water Crisis: A National Problem

The cost of water is increasing at an alarming rate. According to a report by the American Water Works Association, the average U.S. household can expect to see a 7% increase in water rates over the next two years. This translates to an additional $100 per year in water bills, with some cities experiencing even more drastic increases.

  • Boston, MA: Up 15% in 2023, bringing the average monthly water bill to $83.
  • Los Angeles, CA: Up 10% in 2023, bringing the average monthly water bill to $69.
  • Chicago, IL: Up 8% in 2023, bringing the average monthly water bill to $64.

The Impact on Households

For many families, the increased cost of water is a significant burden. With household budgets already stretched thin, the added expense of rising water bills can be devastating. According to a survey by the National Association of Home Builders, 70% of homeowners report that increasing utility costs are a major concern, with 45% stating that they are currently struggling to pay their water bills.
