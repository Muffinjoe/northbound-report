Your Pillow Is Probably Causing Your Pain

Most people don't think of their pillow as a health product. But sleep specialists say it may be the single most important factor in whether you wake up feeling good or in pain.

Here's the issue: standard pillows — whether they're $15 polyester fills or $80 down alternatives — are designed to be soft, not supportive. Within an hour of falling asleep, they compress under the weight of your head, leaving your neck unsupported and your spine misaligned.

Over the course of a full night, this misalignment creates tension that radiates from your neck through your shoulders, upper back, and sometimes all the way to your lower spine. It's the same principle as sitting in a bad chair for eight hours — except you're doing it every single night.

"The vast majority of morning neck and back pain I see in my practice is directly related to sleep posture," explains Dr. Sarah Chen, a board-certified chiropractor in San Diego. "When I ask patients what pillow they're using, the answer is almost always something flat, old, or inappropriate for their sleep position. Fixing the pillow frequently fixes the pain."
